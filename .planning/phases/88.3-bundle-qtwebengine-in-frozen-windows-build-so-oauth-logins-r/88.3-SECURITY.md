## Audit Notes

1. **B1 pivot scope**: Phase 88.3 pivoted from adding WebEngine to the conda bundle (Plan 01 intent) to a B1 isolated-helper-bundle architecture (Plans 02-05). The conda-side `--check-webengine` guard (exit 13) and preflight documented in T-88.3-01's original mitigation plan were deliberately removed. The threat register was updated to reflect the B1 invariants. This audit verifies against the final declared mitigations, not the Plan 01 draft.

2. **Windows-runtime verification**: T-88.3-05-01 is verified exclusively by recorded human UAT (88.3-UAT.md, 4/4 pass). Re-running on Linux is impossible per audit constraints. The recorded evidence is accepted as the human-verification record per phase context.

3. **T-88.3-02 scope migration**: The ICU ABI mismatch risk originally targeted at the conda build env is fully mitigated by the B1 architecture (conda env never installs WebEngine; no ABI mixing possible). The helper-side ABI risk is separately covered by T-88.3-04-02 (SHA256 guard) and T-88.3-04-03 (pinned requirements).

4. **All 26 drift-guard tests confirmed GREEN** per VERIFICATION.md behavioral spot-check (tests/test_packaging_helper_build_spec.py, tests/test_packaging_webengine_spec.py, tests/test_oauth_launch_path.py, tests/test_launcher_frozen_dispatch.py — 26 passed, 1 unrelated warning).

5. **Source paths requiring VM-rebuild re-verification (not in the tested binary)**: Some mitigations are confirmed by static source inspection plus the recorded UAT, but their runtime behavior in the shipped binary was not re-exercised in this audit and would require a VM rebuild + a re-run of UAT tests 1–4 to confirm against a fresh artifact. This applies to the Google login happy-path arm and any build.ps1/.iss source marked UNTOUCHED by the code-review fixer. These are accepted on the strength of the static evidence + the 2026-06-13 UAT (4/4 pass) per the Windows-on-Linux audit constraint; flagged here for traceability rather than treated as open threats.
